Morocan

Moroccan coffee is a unique blend of spices and coffee beans, traditionally served in small cups with a ceremonial pouring ritual. The coffee is brewed with cinnamon, cardamom and sometimes saffron, giving it a distinct and aromatic flavor. It's often served with dates or pastries as a treat. It's an important part of Moroccan culture and hospitality.

Ethiopia

Ethiopian coffee is known for its complex and nuanced flavors, with notes of fruit, spice, and chocolate. It's often considered the birthplace of coffee and is grown on small family-owned farms using traditional methods. The coffee is typically sun-dried and hand-sorted before being exported. Ethiopian coffee is enjoyed in traditional ceremonies and is a symbol of pride and cultural identity for the country. It's one of the most sought-after and respected coffee varieties in the world.

American coffee culture is known for its convenience and fast-paced lifestyle, with drive-thru coffee shops and quick to-go options. Viennese

Vietnamese coffee is a unique blend of dark roast coffee beans and sweetened condensed milk. It is traditionally brewed through a small metal phin filter, which creates a strong and bold flavor. It is often served over ice, making it a perfect drink for hot weather. Vietnamese coffee is a staple in the country's cafes and street vendors and is also gaining popularity in other parts of the world.
